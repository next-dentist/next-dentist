Follow these guidelines before, after, and long-term to ensure the success of your dental implants.

1. Pre-Operative Instructions (Before Surgery)

A. Consultation & Planning

  • Attend all pre-surgical appointments.
  • Provide your full medical history and medications list.
  • Inform your dentist if pregnant, on blood thinners, or have chronic conditions like diabetes.
  • Undergo imaging scans and impressions as needed.

B. Before the Day of Surgery

  • Follow fasting instructions (usually no food/drink for 6–8 hours before surgery).
  • Take prescribed medications as directed.
  • Arrange for someone to drive you home.

C. On the Day of Surgery

  • Wear comfortable clothing.
  • Avoid makeup, jewelry, or contact lenses.
  • Gently brush teeth before coming in.

2. Post-Operative Instructions (After Surgery)

A. First 24 Hours

  • Bite gently on gauze pad for 30–60 minutes to stop bleeding.
  • Apply ice packs (15 min on/off) to reduce swelling.
  • Take medications exactly as prescribed.
  • Avoid spitting, rinsing, or using straws for 24 hours.

B. Diet

  • Eat soft foods (soup, yogurt, mashed potatoes).
  • Stay hydrated with cool or warm liquids.
  • Avoid hot, spicy, crunchy, or chewy foods until cleared by your dentist.

C. Oral Hygiene

  • Avoid brushing near surgical site for first 24 hours.
  • Rinse gently with warm salt water after 24 hours.
  • Brush carefully around area after day one.
  • Use antimicrobial mouthwash if prescribed.

D. Activity

  • Rest and keep head elevated.
  • Avoid strenuous activity for 48–72 hours.
  • Do not smoke for at least 72 hours — ideally quit altogether.

E. Watch for Complications

Contact your dentist immediately if you experience:

  • Excessive bleeding that won’t stop
  • Severe pain not relieved by meds
  • Increasing swelling after 2–3 days
  • Fever or signs of infection
  • Prolonged numbness

3. Healing Period (Osseointegration Phase)

  • Healing usually takes 3–6 months depending on bone quality and implant location.
  • Avoid chewing directly on the implant during healing.
  • Attend all follow-up visits to monitor integration progress.

4. Long-Term Care of Dental Implants

A. Oral Hygiene

  • Brush twice daily with a soft-bristled toothbrush.
  • Floss daily around the implant(s).
  • Use interdental brushes or water flossers as recommended.
  • Continue using antimicrobial rinse if advised.

B. Regular Dental Visits

  • Visit every 6 months or as scheduled for cleanings and checkups.

C. Lifestyle Habits

  • Avoid smoking — increases risk of implant failure.
  • Avoid chewing hard objects like ice or pens.
  • Ask about a night guard if you grind your teeth.
